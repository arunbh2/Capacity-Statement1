ABOUT PRACTICAL ACTION IN SOUTH ASIA

Practical Action is an innovative international development organisation putting ingenious ideas to work across South Asia since the 1980s. With annual income of £30M, we operate as a UK-registered charity with local registrations in multiple South Asian countries including Nepal (1979), Bangladesh (1990), and India (1982).

Our work focuses on four key thematic areas: Energy that Transforms, Farming that Works, Cities Fit for People, and Resilience that Protects. We combine innovative technologies with community-driven approaches to create lasting, sustainable changes.

STRENGTHENING RESILIENCE AT THREE LEVELS

1. ABSORPTIVE CAPACITY
Risk Reduction & Anticipatory Action

  • Early Warning Systems: End-to-end design, last-mile connectivity, gender inclusion, and multi-hazard coverage. In Nepal, increased warning lead time by 5 hours.
  • Disaster Risk Management: Risk-informed planning, institutional capacity building, and standard operating procedures for response.
  • Climate Vulnerability Assessment: Participatory tools, spatial risk mapping, and infrastructure assessment methodologies.
  • Climate-Resilient Infrastructure: Resilient design standards, nature-based solutions, and community management protocols.

2. ADAPTIVE CAPACITY
Climate-Smart Adaptation Planning

  • Nature-Based Solutions: Urban ecosystem services, green-gray integration, participatory design, carbon sequestration co-benefits, and ecological restoration.
  • Climate-Resilient WASH: Climate-proofed design, diversified water sources, decentralized solutions, and inclusive service delivery models.
  • Innovative Financing: Risk transfer mechanisms, community-based savings, blended finance models. Successfully implemented flood insurance for 935 farmers through 12 cooperatives.
  • Circular Resource Systems: Waste-to-resource models, water reuse, value chain development, and low-carbon materials for sustainable urban environments.

3. TRANSFORMATIVE CAPACITY
Scaled Decentralized Solutions

  • Policy Development: Climate-integrated planning, decentralized decision-making, rights-based approaches, and cross-sectoral coordination frameworks.
  • Institutional Capacity: Technical training, knowledge management, stakeholder engagement, and financial management for local authorities.
  • Evidence-Based Decision Making: Local climate information systems, impact evaluation, cost-benefit analysis, and learning platforms for continuous improvement.
  • Cross-Sectoral Integration: Multi-stakeholder platforms, integrated planning, and collaborative implementation mechanisms for cohesive approaches.

NEPAL EXPERIENCE (Since 1979)

Multi-Hazard Early Warning Systems
  • Community-centric EWS for Landslides (2023-25): Protecting 52,420 people in Bagmati Province through rainfall thresholds and community protocols
  • EWS for Hydro-Meteorological Disaster (2022-24): Benefiting 59,472 people directly and 137,636 indirectly through strengthened local flood early warning
  • Zurich Flood Resilience Alliance (2013-24): Building community resilience in Bardiya and Kailali districts through assessment frameworks and local action
Innovative Finance & Urban Resilience
  • Index-Based Flood Insurance (2021-ongoing): Parametric insurance for 935 farmers through 12 cooperatives covering 134 hectares of paddy fields
  • Building Disaster Resilient Communities (2012-14): Implemented in Pokhara Sub-Metropolitan City with local government bodies and civil society
  • BISWASH - Building Back Better (2016-17): Enhanced urban resilience in Panauti Municipality focusing on WASH systems

BANGLADESH EXPERIENCE (Since 1990)

Climate Resilience Measurement
  • Climate Resilience Measurement for Communities (CRMC): Implementing framework in Faridpur, Satkhira, and Khulna until 2035, addressing multiple hazards including floods, heatwaves, and wildfires
  • Zurich Climate Resilience Alliance: Positively impacted approximately 250,000 individuals with enhanced disaster resilience capabilities
Locally Led Adaptation
  • People's Adaptation Plan (2025-26): Developing inclusive plans for Narayanganj City Corporation, integrated with ADB's US$155M 'Narayanganj Green and Resilient Urban Master Plan'
  • Ecosystem-based Adaptation (2023-27): Working in the Sundarbans and Hakaluki Haor for climate resilience through nature-based adaptation solutions
Digital Innovation in Early Warning
  • 'Disaster Alert for Bangladesh' (2023-25): Mobile application developed with the Ministry of Disaster Management and Relief for improved early warning dissemination
  • Youth Engagement in EWS (2024-26): Building capacity of youth volunteers in climate hazardous areas as resilience agents
